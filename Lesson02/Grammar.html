nouns

A noun represents a person, place, animal, thing or an idea.

kinds of noun

Nouns are classified as follows:
  1. Proper: It is the name of a particular object, whether place, person, animal, idea or thing.
  2. Common: It is the name possessed by things in general.
  3. Collective: It is the name representing a group of things.
  4. Abstract: It is the name representing qualities, emotions, conditions or actions.

pronouns


A pronoun is a word used in place of a noun. A pronoun is generally used to avoid repetition of a noun.

kinds of pronoun


Pronouns are classified as follows:
  1. Personal pronouns: It refers to a specific noun and used as a substitude of the noun.
  2. Interrogative pronouns: It stands as the answer to a question about a person, place or thing.
  3. Relative pronouns: It relates to a noun, pronoun, word or expression preceeding it in the sentence, and at the same time connects two statements.
  4. Demonstrative pronouns: A noun or pronoun is specified and identified by it.
  5. Reflexive pronouns: It usually follows a noun or pronoun and used to emphasize the subject.
